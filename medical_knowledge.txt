Hemoglobin:
Normal range: 13.5-17.5 g/dL for men, 12.0-15.5 g/dL for women
Low hemoglobin (below 7) indicates severe anemia, requires immediate medical attention
High hemoglobin (above 20) may indicate polycythemia, requires medical evaluation
Common causes of low hemoglobin: iron deficiency, vitamin B12 deficiency, blood loss

Glucose:
Normal range: 70-100 mg/dL fasting, below 140 mg/dL after meals
Low glucose (below 50) indicates hypoglycemia, can cause unconsciousness, emergency
High glucose (above 200) indicates possible diabetes or hyperglycemia, requires medical attention
Pre-diabetes range: 100-125 mg/dL fasting

WBC (White Blood Cells):
Normal range: 4,500-11,000 /µL
Low WBC (below 2000) indicates possible immune deficiency or bone marrow problem
High WBC (above 30,000) may indicate infection, inflammation, or leukemia
Mild elevation 11,000-30,000 often indicates infection or stress

Cholesterol:
Normal total cholesterol: below 200 mg/dL
Borderline high: 200-239 mg/dL
High: above 240 mg/dL
High cholesterol increases risk of heart disease and stroke

Platelets:
Normal range: 150,000-400,000 /µL
Low platelets (below 50,000) indicates thrombocytopenia, risk of dangerous bleeding
High platelets (above 1,000,000) indicates thrombocytosis, risk of clotting
Mild low platelets 50,000-150,000 requires monitoring

Blood Pressure:
Normal: below 120/80 mmHg
Elevated: 120-129 systolic
High Stage 1: 130-139 systolic
High Stage 2: 140 or higher systolic
Hypertensive crisis: above 180 systolic, requires immediate medical attention

Creatinine:
Normal range: 0.7-1.3 mg/dL for men, 0.6-1.1 mg/dL for women
High creatinine indicates possible kidney dysfunction
Very high creatinine (above 10) may indicate kidney failure

Vitamin D:
Normal range: 20-50 ng/mL
Deficient: below 12 ng/mL
Insufficient: 12-20 ng/mL
High dose supplementation needed below 20 ng/mL

Iron:
Normal range: 60-170 µg/dL for men, 50-170 µg/dL for women
Low iron causes fatigue, weakness, and anemia
High iron can damage organs over time

ALT (Alanine Aminotransferase):
Normal range: 7-56 U/L for men, 7-45 U/L for women
High ALT indicates liver damage or inflammation
Very high ALT (above 200) may indicate hepatitis or severe liver damage
Common causes: fatty liver, hepatitis, alcohol, medications

AST (Aspartate Aminotransferase):
Normal range: 10-40 U/L
High AST indicates liver or heart muscle damage
Very high AST (above 200) may indicate severe liver damage or heart attack
Often checked alongside ALT for liver health assessment

Bilirubin:
Normal total bilirubin: 0.1-1.2 mg/dL
High bilirubin causes jaundice (yellowing of skin and eyes)
Very high bilirubin (above 3) requires immediate medical attention
Causes: liver disease, bile duct obstruction, hemolysis

Creatinine:
Normal range: 0.7-1.3 mg/dL for men, 0.6-1.1 mg/dL for women
High creatinine indicates kidney dysfunction
Very high creatinine (above 10) may indicate kidney failure
Kidneys filter creatinine so high levels mean kidneys are not working well

Urea (BUN - Blood Urea Nitrogen):
Normal range: 7-20 mg/dL
High urea indicates kidney dysfunction or dehydration
Very high urea (above 50) requires medical attention
Low urea may indicate malnutrition or liver disease

TSH (Thyroid Stimulating Hormone):
Normal range: 0.4-4.0 mIU/L
High TSH indicates hypothyroidism (underactive thyroid)
Low TSH indicates hyperthyroidism (overactive thyroid)
Thyroid controls metabolism, energy, and body temperature

T3 (Triiodothyronine):
Normal range: 80-200 ng/dL
Low T3 indicates hypothyroidism
High T3 indicates hyperthyroidism
Works with T4 to regulate body metabolism

T4 (Thyroxine):
Normal range: 5.0-12.0 µg/dL
Low T4 indicates hypothyroidism
High T4 indicates hyperthyroidism
Main hormone produced by thyroid gland

Vitamin B12:
Normal range: 200-900 pg/mL
Low B12 (below 200) causes nerve damage and anemia
Very low B12 (below 100) requires immediate treatment
Common in vegetarians and elderly people

Iron (Serum Iron):
Normal range: 60-170 µg/dL for men, 50-170 µg/dL for women
Low iron causes iron deficiency anemia
High iron may indicate hemochromatosis
Iron is essential for hemoglobin production

Ferritin:
Normal range: 20-500 ng/mL for men, 20-200 ng/mL for women
Low ferritin (below 12) indicates iron deficiency
High ferritin may indicate inflammation or liver disease
Ferritin stores iron in the body

CRP (C-Reactive Protein):
Normal range: below 1.0 mg/L (low risk)
1.0-3.0 mg/L indicates moderate inflammation risk
Above 3.0 mg/L indicates high inflammation or infection
Very high CRP (above 10) indicates acute infection or inflammation

ESR (Erythrocyte Sedimentation Rate):
Normal range: 0-22 mm/hr for men, 0-29 mm/hr for women
High ESR indicates inflammation, infection, or autoimmune disease
Very high ESR (above 100) requires further investigation
Non-specific marker used alongside other tests

HbA1c (Glycated Hemoglobin):
Normal range: below 5.7%
Pre-diabetes: 5.7-6.4%
Diabetes: 6.5% and above
Measures average blood sugar over past 3 months

Uric Acid:
Normal range: 3.5-7.2 mg/dL for men, 2.6-6.0 mg/dL for women
High uric acid causes gout and kidney stones
Very high uric acid (above 10) requires medical attention
Produced by breakdown of purines in food

Sodium:
Normal range: 135-145 mEq/L
Low sodium (below 135) indicates hyponatremia — causes confusion and fatigue
High sodium (above 145) indicates hypernatremia — causes thirst and confusion
Critical for fluid balance and nerve function

Potassium:
Normal range: 3.5-5.0 mEq/L
Low potassium (below 3.0) causes muscle weakness and heart problems
High potassium (above 6.0) is dangerous for heart rhythm
Critical for heart and muscle function

Calcium:
Normal range: 8.5-10.5 mg/dL
Low calcium causes muscle cramps and bone weakness
High calcium (above 12) may indicate parathyroid problems or cancer
Essential for bones, muscles, and nerve function

Albumin:
Normal range: 3.5-5.0 g/dL
Low albumin indicates malnutrition or liver disease
Main protein in blood that carries nutrients
Low levels indicate poor overall health status

PSA (Prostate Specific Antigen):
Normal range: below 4.0 ng/mL for men
High PSA may indicate prostate enlargement or cancer
Requires follow-up with urologist if elevated
Only applicable for men

eGFR (Estimated Glomerular Filtration Rate):
Normal range: above 60 mL/min/1.73m²
Below 60 indicates chronic kidney disease
Below 15 indicates kidney failure
Measures how well kidneys filter waste
